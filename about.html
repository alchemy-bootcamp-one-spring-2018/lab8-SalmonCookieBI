It all started with a fish.

Saumon's Cookies was founded in 1902 by a French-American baker named Simone Saumon, who was lured to the Pacific Northwest by the quality of the salmon in the region.

Simone set up a small shop in the Pearl and got to work. Using only the freshest ingredients available, they began to make a number of different baked goods, incorporating the local salmon into every recipe.

These unique cookies and pastries tapped into a new market, one that no one else had even considered before. Who knew that combining fish and baked goods would be so popular? It was a testament to Saumon's ingenuity and revolutionary forward-thinking that made Saumon's Cookies so popular. Simone did not just disrupt baking; they invented a whole new kind of baked good.

Since then, Saumon's Cookies has flourished, expanding to five locations in Portland. Future plans include opening an informal cafe, the Cafe Salmonella, at the Pearl location by 2020.
